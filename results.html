Our Results and Evaluations

User Experience

We evaluated the search engine interface by asking friends and family what they thought about it. I asked them to each use the website to find a specific drink. Four out of the five people found the drink they were looking for and that was before we crawled a bunch of other websites. As far as the web interface went, they all thought it was exciting, intuitive, and easy to use. I got particular praise about the slideshow in the background, the design and the bottle cap buttons. I then got each of them to access the website through their phones. This they said could be better. They disliked that the design and buttons became small, but they still said it worked fine and could they still navigate it.

P@N Test

To test our research results, we used the P@N method to test the relevance of the first N results. We used N = 5 for instant relevancy. For the queries:

“long island iced tea”: 2/5 relevant results
“irish car bomb”: 4/5 relevant results
“rum”: 2/5 relevant results
“Christmas”: 3/5 relevant results

Our results in general are not very relevant, but not irrelevant.

User Testing

We also tested our search results with casually potential users (college students). We asked users to search for an alcoholic drink in general, and tell us what they think about the results. The most common outcome was, people could find something relevant to what they were searching for, but there is a lot of other search result noise that were irrelevant. In conclusion, there is room for Let’s Drink to grow to provide better results for their users.

Audience

We have seen positive growth in our audience. More and more people are visiting Let’s Drink. Over half of our audience are new users, while the other half are returning users. Also people stay on Let’s Drink for almost 4 minutes, so we can assume that Let’s Drink is relevant to our users.

Audience Acquisition

We have implemented keywords into our html tag so that we can be better found by search engines. As seen in the Acquisition report, we are able to be found from organic search. But also people are directly finding us from word of mouth and sharing Let’s Drink directly with users. But also we have been found from referrals via social media sites.
